DAD-TAD: Diffusion Action Distiller for Temporal Action Detection

Temporal Action Detection (TAD) in untrimmed videos remains a challenging task due to the complexity of pinpointing action boundaries within noise-laden dynamic sequences. This paper tackles this challenge by introducing a novel two-stage action boundary refinement mechanism dubbed DAD-TAD, for accurately localizing the action boundaries from dynamic noises. DAD-TAD comprises two main components including an Initial Ation Locator(IAL) and a Diffusion-based Boundary Refiner(DiffBR). The former mainly composed of newly proposed ConvFormer blocks that aggregate multi-scale dynamic features, as well as a multi-scale head to generate initial action proposals. Subsequently, the initial action boundaries will be refined by first diffusing the boundary locations and then reversing this process (i.e. the noising and denoising processes of diffusion model). Meanwhile, By vectorizing boundaries to mimic thermodynamic distributions, the diffusion model aims to reduce uncertainty between the initial action boundaries and the ground truth. Experimentally, the proposed DAD-TAD outperforms other state-of-the-art methods in action localization task on four publicly available datasets including THUMOS14, ActivityNet-V1.3 and EPIC-KITCHEN 100.
