SelfMimic: Learning Robust and Symmetric Locomotion Skills for Biped Robots

Anonymous authors     Submitted to IEEE Transactions on Automation Science and Engineering

Abstract

Achieving human-like gait symmetry for biped robots remains challenging due to two inherent limitations: high-dimensional nonlinear dynamics of multijoint coordination and computational complexity of real-time balance stabilization under environmental perturbations. Although previous methods using explicit symmetry constraints partially address the two issues, they suffer from low robustness to unexpected disturbances and training inefficiency caused by complex reward engineering. We propose SelfMimic, a lightweight framework that synergizes implicit bilateral symmetry constraints with gated multilayer perceptrons (gMLPs) for biped locomotion. SelfMimic achieves actuator-level symmetry through reciprocal imitation between contralateral limbs, eliminating the need for predefined trajectories or dense reward engineering. gMLP enables efficient temporal interaction modeling while maintaining computational tractability for real-time deployment. Experiments in both simulated and real-world environments demonstrate that SelfMimic achieves robust locomotion and fast policy convergence, while generating anthropomorphic locomotion behaviors.
