RoboTrio: Hierarchical Whole-Body Control of
Wheeled-Quadrupedal Manipulators for
Long-Range Flexible 6D End-Effector Tracking


  • Hao Ju
  • Shuo Yang
  • Zhiqiang Yang
  • Fuxiang Feng
  • Jinqiu Fan
  • Wei Zhang
    • Shandong University

Abstract

Long-range flexible 6D end-effector (EE) tracking is a practical yet challenging problem, requiring the robot to simultaneously achieve stable and efficient long-range target approach, smooth locomotion-manipulation transition, accurate 6D EE pose tracking, and flexible switching between conventional and posture-conditioned EE tracking. Existing methods rarely satisfy all these requirements. To address this problem, we propose RoboTrio, a hierarchical framework with a two-stage training scheme. Low-level policies are first trained separately to develop strong motion and manipulation capabilities, and a high-level policy is then learned for unified whole-body control. We further introduce Adaptive Reward Integration to improve target approach efficiency and phase transition smoothness. Together with conditional commands, RoboTrio enables long-range flexible 6D EE tracking within a unified policy. Simulation results verify that RoboTrio effectively accomplishes this task. Real-world experiments further show that this capability leads to substantially higher success rates in both open and posture-constrained mobile manipulation tasks. In challenging real-world tasks, RoboTrio achieves success rates above 60\%, significantly outperforming baseline methods.
