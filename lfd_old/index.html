From explicit to implicit: Investigate video content change for robot imitation learning

Shuo Yang, Wei Zhang, Weizhi Lu, Hesheng Wang, and Yibin Li

Abstract

Demonstration understanding is the vital component for robot imitation learning. In this article, we investigate the change-based representation of demonstration and utilize the representation to build imitation learning system in explicit and implicit ways. First, we propose a novel representation named content change map and utilize it to build imitation learning system in explicit way. To pursue a more "human-like" imitation learning system, we try to extend the change-based representation from image-level to feature-level and build full imitation learning system implicitly. We conduct extensive experiments to evaluate our proposed change-based representation and the experimental results verify the effectiveness and advantages of our methods. Finally, the comparative experimental results between the explicit and implicit methods demonstrate the superiority of implicit way for robot imitation learning.
