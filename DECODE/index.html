DECODE: Decoupled-oriented Denoising Model for Category-agnostic Pose Estimation

Zhiheng Li, Tianjiao Li, Mingxin Zhang, Ran Song*, Jiyu Cheng, Yonghuai Liu, and Wei Zhang
School of Control Science and Engineering, Shandong University

Abstract

Category-agnostic pose estimation (CAPE) aims to predict keypoints for novel categories with the support of few annotated samples. Existing methods usually operate by constructing a feature space that jointly represents keypoints and images, and establish mappings from this space to query keypoints. However, such mappings can be easily disrupted by appearance and structure discrepancies between support and query samples, and the joint latent space exhibits significant distribution bias due to the heterogeneity of keypoint and image modalities. To address the two issues, we first formulate such discrepancies as intrinsic noises and propose the DECOupled-oriented DEnoising model (DECODE) to restore the pristine feature space. Also, DECODE decouples the joint feature space and conducts the conditional diffusion process on the independent features respectively, thus avoiding the distribution bias. Additionally, we introduce a gating-based composition layer that adaptively integrates the separated features by assessing the appearance and the structure dissimilarities between support and query features. In the experiments, we propose a new noise adversarial test to quantitatively evaluate the robustness of different methods against real-world noises. The results demonstrate that DECODE outperforms the state-of-the-art methods for CAPE on the MP-100 dataset..
