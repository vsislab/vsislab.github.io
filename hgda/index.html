Human-Guided Data Augmentation via Diffusion Model for Surface Defect Recognition Under Limited Data

Tiyu Fang1, Mingxin Zhang1, Ran Song1*, Xiaolei Li1, Zhiyuan Wei1, Wei Zhang1
1 School of Control Science and Engineering, Shandong University

Abstract

Surface defect recognition (SDR) plays an important role in industrial production. Although existing SDR methods have shown promising results, they usually require adequate defect samples. However, only a limited number of defect samples are available in most industrial systems, which poses a great challenge in real-world applications. To address the issue, this paper proposes a human-guided data augmentation method for SDR. Its core idea is to introduce human feedback into a diffusion model for synthesizing controllable and high-quality defect samples under extremely limited data by reinforcement learning (RL). First, a conditional diffusion model (CDM) is constructed to generate controllable defect samples using semantic labels, which learns defect distribution from a small number of annotated defect samples. Then, a reward model is designed to evaluate the outcome of CDM by human feedback. Next, based on the trained reward model, CDM is further optimized by proximal policy optimization (PPO). Finally, the refined diffusion model is used to generate high-quality defect samples for improving SDR. Extensive experiments on NEU-Seg, MT and the collected Tire datasets demonstrate the superiority of our method. Code and Datasets Available Here
