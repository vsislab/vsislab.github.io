A Generalist Agent Learning Architecture for Versatile Quadruped Locomotion

Abstract

Humanoids increasingly demand a single policy capable of generalizing across diverse motion categories and deployment scenarios. However, most reinforcement imitation learning-based approaches formulate motion tracking as an instantaneous alignment problem coupled with motion-agnostic smoothness regularization, which limits whole-body expressiveness and cross-scenario adaptability of humanoid robots. To address this issue, we propose the generalist motion policy (GMP) for humanoid whole-body control (WBC). It not only pursues high instantaneous tracking accuracy, but also allows appropriate action adjustment that deviates from the reference trajectory, improving generalization across diverse motions. Moreover, \OurS replaces smoothness regularization based on instantaneous state variations with motion-dependent regularization based on frequency-domain alignment, which both suppresses high-frequency jitter in smooth motions and tracks highly dynamic motions well. Extensive experiments in simulated and real-world environments demonstrate that GMP achieves good cross-task generalization and outperforms a set of state-of-the-art methods for humanoid WBC.
