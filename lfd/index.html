From explicit to implicit: Investigate video content change for robot imitation learning

Shuo Yang1, Wei Zhang1*, Weizhi Lu1, Hesheng Wang2, and Yibin Li1
1School of Control Science and Engineering, Shandong University  2Department of Automation, Shanghai Jiao Tong University

Abstract

Demonstration understanding is the vital component for robot imitation learning. In this work, we investigate the content change based representation of the demonstration and build imitation learning system in both explicit and implicit ways. Specifically, we first propose to represent demonstration video via content change map, and utilize it to generate explicit command for robot execution. To pursue a more "human-like" imitation learning system, an implicit method is presented by extending the content change based representation from image level to feature level. Extensive experiments are conducted to evaluate the proposed methods, and the results show that the proposed content change based approaches outperform state-of-the-art imitation learning methods significantly. Also, the results indicate the superiority of the implicit method over the explicit one for imitation learning.
