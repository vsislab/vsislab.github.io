SEIL: Imitating versatile whole body control with skill encoding for humanoid robot

Abstract

With the high-dimensional nonlinear dynamic constraints of multi-joint coordination of the humanoid robots, existing methods based on explicit constraints are out of balancing the flexibility of motions and failed to learn diverse whole-body motion skills, simultaneously. To address this issue, this paper proposed a two-stage imitation learning algorithm by integrating skill encoding with human video motion sequences (SEIL). In the first stage, SEIL introduced a motion evaluation mechanism based on the physical quantity estimation and contact information to effectively filter and correct the reference trajectories. Then, it utilizes the differential inverse kinematics to re-target the reference trajectories to the G1 robot and builds a strategy library, which contains a set of human-like motion skills through multi-policy optimization. In the second stage, SEIL trains a neural state machine via reinforcement learning to encode and integrate the pre-trained human-like motion strategies, achieving dynamic execution and smooth switching of motion sequences for the biped robot. Experimental results in both simulated and real world environments demonstrate that SEIL can generate ergonomic symmetric gaits and diverse human-like motion skills, effectively improving the robustness of biped robot motion control and human-like motion performance.
