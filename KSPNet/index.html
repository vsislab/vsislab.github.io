Knowledge-Sharing Prompt Network for Continual Defect Segmentation

Tiyu Fang1, Lin Zhang1, Ran Song1*, Yongqing Ye1, Xiaolei Li1, Wei Zhang1
1 School of Control Science and Engineering, Shandong University

Abstract

Defect segmentation is crucial in industrial quality inspection, with deep learning methods becoming the mainstream solution. In practical production, defect data accumulates over time, and new defect categories may appear at any time. However, existing models often suffer from catastrophic forgetting when adapting to new categories, causing significant accuracy degradation on old categories and impairing overall segmentation performance. To address this issue, continual defect segmentation (CDS) is introduced in this paper, and a knowledge-sharing prompt network (KSPNet) is proposed to balance the knowledge of new and old defect categories for accurate CDS. Specifically, the proposed KSPNet preserves existing knowledge by freezing the backbone network and adapts to new categories by fine-tuning the prompt embedding, effectively alleviating catastrophic forgetting. In the processing, a Knowledge-Balanced Low-Rank Adaptation (KB-LoRA) method is designed to incorporate multi-task low-rank matrices into frozen image encoder for joint learning of defect categories under the incremental training setting, and the loss constraints across multiple tasks are introduced to ensure efficient knowledge retention of new and old defect categories. Meantime, a Complementary Prompt Block(CPBlock) is designed to introduce general and task-specific prompts for adaptive learning to obtain complementary prompt representations for accurate defect segmentation. Extensive experiments conducted on the constructed CID-Seg and WD-Seg datasets have shown that the proposed method significantly outperforms existing methods and validates its superiority in the CDS task.
