ViV-ReID: Bidirectional Structural-Aware Spatial-Temporal Graph Networks on Large-Scale Video-Based vessel Re-Identification Dataset

Mingxin Zhang1, Fuxiang Feng1, Xing Fang1,Youmei Zhang1,2, Wei Zhang1
1 School of Control Science and Engineering, Shandong University 2 School of Mathematics and Statistics, Qilu University of Technology (Shandong Academy of Sciences)

Abstract

Vessel re-identification (Re-ID) serves as a foundational pillar for intelligent maritime transportation systems. Current research predominantly focuses on image-based methods, leaving video-based vessel Re-ID underexplored. To bridge this gap, we introduce ViV-ReID, the first publicly available large-scale video-based vessel Re-ID dataset, comprising 480 vessel identities captured from 20 cross-port camera views (7,165 tracklets and 1.14 million frames), establishing a benchmark for advancing vessel Re-ID from image to video processing. We further propose a Bidirectional Structural-Aware Spatial-Temporal Graph Network (Bi-SSTN) that explicitly aligns spatiotemporal features using vessel structural priors. Extensive experiments demonstrate that Bi-SASTN achieves significant performance improvements over state-of-the-art methods on ViV-ReID, validating its effectiveness in modeling vessel-specific spatio-temporal patterns.
